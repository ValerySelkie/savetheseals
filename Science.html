Scientists have found the meaning of life

A group of philosophy scientists from Oklahoma State University seem to have figured out what is the whole point of our existence as human beings. Philosophy experts from all around the world call this an international sensation and the most important discovery of the century.

Meet Brian Lolz. He is a philosophy master at Oklahoma State University and is dedicated to his career, as he says, exclusively and entirely. "I remember one time me, my wife and my seven kids were short of money because my wage is 100$ lover than the minimum wage in this country, and my wife begged me to use the 40$ that we had left to buy some food for the children, but I told her: 'No, woman, I am spending it on a 600-page book about Plato's left foot!'. And so I did, because nothing can stand in the way of my intellectual fulfillment!" - says Brian with a smile. He later admitted that his kids got taken away from him the next day by the Child Protective Services. He didn't seem particularly happy about it.

Brian and his team of researchers have spent two years studying Plato's, Wolter's and Homer's works, conducting experiments on cockroaches and chewing philosophical stones provided to them by Montana Mythology Association, in search for an answer to one big question: What is the meaning of life? "We barely ate anything," - admits Brian's colleague Peter Vicktem, - "Brian kept us in the room with doors locked, forcing us to physically and emotionally drain ourselves in the name of science. In the end Johnny [another colleague] was so exhausted that he started chewing on my shoe thinking it was an eggplant,". Many people from the university staff complained about weird noises coming from the classroom at 3AM every night. "They were chanting some kind of a weird song. - complains Mike Schmidt, an Oklahoma State University night guard. - They seemed to have been worshiping someone named Shaggy, but I'm not sure,".

Today, months of research later, Brian and his team finally are proud to announce the result of their hard work. "The meaning of life... - says Brian with fanatism written all over his face, - is ice-cream! The whole center of our existence, the goal of our lives is finding the ice-cream type that is perfect for us. We spend our days tasting endless portions of this frozen dessert in search for the true match, the one and only, the perfect one. And only once we find it we can become perfectly happy!".

Brian's discovery has had a huge resonance in the public. "This is the most genius thing I have ever heard! - exclaims Harold Smart, a president of Global Science Association. - Mr. Lolz should absolutely be nominated for Nobel Prizes in all of the existing sciences, all of my colleagues agree with me on this one!". Governments of Sweden and France have shown their appreciation for the discovery by providing endless ice-cream coupons to all the senior citizens of the countries and investing the government money into developing the ice-cream producing industry.
